A standard architectural shingle roof lasts 20 to 30 years with proper installation and attic ventilation. Standing seam metal roofs regularly last 50+ years, while flat roofs (TPO) last 20 to 30 years. Lifespan is highly dependent on regular maintenance and storm exposure.

If shingle damage is localized (a few missing shingles or a cracked pipe boot), repair is sufficient. If shingles are curling, cupping, or losing granules across multiple sections, or if the roof is over 15 to 20 years old, a complete replacement is more cost-effective.

Choosing a local contractor like Ramos Roofing Plus ensures long-term support. Out-of-town roofers disappear after storm events, making it impossible to contact them for warranty issues. We are a family-owned business operating locally for over 25 years.

Yes, we offer flexible financing options for residential roof replacements, including low-monthly-payment plans and short-term interest-free options. Financing can bridge the gap while waiting for insurance payouts or make a premium upgrade affordable.

Most standard residential roof replacements are completed in just 1 to 2 workdays. Our crews tear off the old shingles, inspect and repair decking, install synthetic underlayment, apply the new shingles or metal panels, and perform a magnetic sweep for nails.

We cover lawns, gardens, and decks with heavy-duty tarps. We build wooden shields over AC units and windows. After completing the work, we perform a thorough cleanup and run magnets to capture all stray nails, ensuring your property is left clean.

Class 4 shingles are modified with rubberized polymers that absorb the impact of hail up to 2 inches in size without fracturing. Installing Class 4 shingles protects your roof against spring storms and often qualifies you for insurance premium discounts.

No. When installed over solid roof decking with high-quality underlayment and attic insulation, a residential metal roof is no noisier than a traditional shingle roof during rain. The solid decking and insulation dampen the sound, ensuring a quiet interior.

While building codes may allow a two-layer overlay, we do not recommend it. An overlay prevents us from inspecting the decking for rot, traps heat and moisture, adds excessive weight, and often voids manufacturer warranties. We always recommend tearing off.

We partner with leading manufacturers, including TAMKO and Malarkey. We recommend TAMKO Titan XT shingles for high-wind areas due to their reinforced nail zone, and Malarkey Legacy shingles for maximum hail protection due to their rubberized asphalt.

Without balanced ventilation (soffit intake and ridge exhaust), hot air bakes your shingles from underneath, shortening their lifespan and voiding warranties. Ventilation also reduces attic temperatures, lowering your summer cooling bills.

A pipe boot is a rubber and metal flashing installed around plumbing vent pipes. The rubber collar is exposed to UV rays, causing it to crack and dry out within 5 to 8 years, long before shingles wear out. Replacing deteriorated boots is a common maintenance task.

Black streaks are caused by blue-green algae that thrives in humid climates. The algae feeds on the limestone filler in shingles. We install shingles with copper-containing granules that naturally inhibit algae growth, keeping your roof clean.

We recommend having your roof inspected at least once a year, preferably in the fall, or immediately following any severe storm events. Regular inspections allow you to catch minor issues like cracked pipe boots or loose shingles before they turn into leaks.

Yes, we provide 24/7 emergency tarping and patching. If high winds or fallen branches cause active leaks, call us at (479) 652-1424. We will dispatch a crew to install temporary waterproof tarps to protect your home's interior until permanent repairs can be scheduled.

Hail damage leaves circular bruised indentations where granules are missing, exposing the asphalt mat. Wind damage causes shingles to lift, crease, or tear. Our trained inspectors perform physical checks to identify these storm markers.

We specialize in single-ply TPO white membranes, multi-ply modified bitumen systems, standing seam commercial metal roofing, and elastomeric silicone coatings. We help you choose the best system based on your building's slope and budget.

Standing seam metal roofs use hidden clips and fasteners underneath the panels, eliminating exposed screws that can leak. This makes standing seam ideal for residential roofs. Exposed-fastener rib panels are secured with screws, offering a cost-effective barn option.

TPO is highly reflective, bouncing up to 80% of solar heat away from your building. This reduces the thermal load, resulting in significantly lower cooling costs and extending the operational lifespan of your building's HVAC systems.

Yes, if your commercial roof is structurally sound but leaking, we can apply an elastomeric silicone coating. This liquid-applied coating seals all seams, penetrations, and micro-cracks, creating a seamless waterproof membrane at a fraction of tear-off costs.

We offer dual warranty protection: a manufacturer's material warranty (typically a lifetime warranty on premium shingles) and our workmanship warranty. Our certified status also qualifies you for extended manufacturer systems warranties.

If your roof requires replacement due to covered storm damage (like wind blow-offs or hail strikes), the cost is typically covered by insurance. We assist by providing detailed photo reports and estimates, and we meet adjusters on-site to document all damage.

We install premium siding materials, including James Hardie fiber cement siding, LP SmartSide engineered wood, insulated vinyl siding, and traditional vinyl panels. We help you choose the best material based on your budget and aesthetic preferences.

Vinyl siding is cost-effective and low-maintenance. James Hardie fiber cement siding offers superior durability, resists rot, fire, pests, and impact from hail, and holds paint much longer than wood, providing lifetime durability for your home.

We tear off the old siding, inspect the sheathing for rot, and repair any compromised boards. We then install a high-performance house wrap and seal all joints with flashing tape, creating a weather-resistant barrier before the siding is applied.

Yes, coordinating a siding replacement and a roof replacement is highly efficient. Replacing both systems at the same time ensures a complete weather-tight seal for your home's exterior envelope and saves you money on consolidated labor costs.

Yes, we custom-miter seamless aluminum gutters on-site for a leak-free fit. We also install gutter guards to prevent leaves and pine needles from clogging your gutters, which is essential to protect your foundation and eave boards.
