Frequently Asked Questions

Roof color has a significant impact on heat absorption. Dark shingles (such as Charcoal or dark brown) absorb up to 90% of solar radiation, transferring it into your attic and raising cooling bills during hot Arkansas summers. In contrast, light-colored shingles (such as white or light gray) and reflective metal roofs bounce solar heat away from your home, maintaining cooler attic temperatures and reducing HVAC system stress.

In the Fort Smith and NWA regions, classic neutral tones are highly popular. Weathered Wood, Charcoal, and Slate Gray are the top choices for architectural shingle replacements. These colors match traditional brick, vinyl siding, and natural stone styles common in Arkansas neighborhoods. We also offer designer colors like Hunter Green and Colonial Red for custom-built homes.

Yes, constant exposure to UV rays causes mineral granules on shingles to slowly fade over time. To prevent fading, we partner with top manufacturers like TAMKO and Malarkey, who use advanced ceramic-coated granules. These granules are fired at high temperatures to lock in color and resist sun bleaching, ensuring your roof retains its rich color for decades.

Matching your roof to your home's exterior requires balancing contrast. If your home has warm-toned brick (red, tan, or brown), warm shingle colors like Weathered Wood or Chestnut are ideal. If your home has cool-toned siding (white, gray, or blue), cool shingle colors like Charcoal or Slate Gray look best. We provide physical color boards and digital tools to help you visualize options.

Gloeocapsa magma is a blue-green algae that thrives in humid climates like the Arkansas River Valley. The algae feeds on the limestone filler used in asphalt shingles, leaving dark, unsightly streaks that ruin your home's curb appeal. We install shingles with copper-containing granules that naturally inhibit algae growth, preventing streaks and keeping your roof clean.

Yes, standing seam metal roofs offer an extensive range of custom colors. Popular choices include modern Matte Black, Bronze, Slate Gray, Forest Green, and copper finishes. We use steel panels coated with premium PVDF/Kynar paint systems, which offer outstanding resistance to fade, chalk, and rust, keeping your metal roof looking vibrant.

Yes, we install specialized reflective shingles that meet 'cool roof' standards. These shingles incorporate reflective granules that bounce solar heat away from your home. Upgrading to a cool roof shingle keeps your home comfortable, reduces cooling costs, and meets strict environmental codes without requiring you to switch to a white metal or flat roof system.
