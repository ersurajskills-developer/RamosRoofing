Frequently Asked Questions

We offer a wide selection of premium siding materials, including James Hardie fiber cement siding, LP SmartSide engineered wood, insulated vinyl siding, and traditional vinyl panels. We help you choose the best material based on your budget, energy efficiency needs, maintenance preferences, and architectural style.

Standard vinyl siding lasts 20 to 30 years with minimal maintenance. James Hardie fiber cement siding is exceptionally durable, offering a 50+ year operational lifespan. Fiber cement resists rot, fire, pests, and severe wind/hail impacts much better than vinyl, making it a premium long-term choice.

Yes, insulated vinyl siding features a contoured foam backing that provides a continuous thermal barrier. This backing blocks 'thermal bridging' (heat loss through wall studs), significantly increasing your home's R-value. Insulated siding helps maintain comfortable indoor temperatures and lowers your heating and cooling costs.

Preparation is key to a water-tight siding installation. We tear off the old siding, inspect the sheathing for rot or moisture damage, and repair any compromised boards. We then install a high-performance house wrap (like Tyvek) and seal all joints with specialized flashing tape, creating a weather-resistant barrier.

Fiber cement siding requires very little maintenance. Unlike wood, it resists rot and pests. It features baked-on ColorPlus finishes that resist fading, requiring repainting only every 15 years. An annual wash with a garden hose and soft brush keeps it looking clean and vibrant.

While it is possible to install vinyl siding over old wood panels, we do not recommend it. An overlay traps existing moisture and rot, makes it impossible to inspect wall sheathing, and adds weight. Tearing off old siding ensures a flat sheathing surface and validates manufacturer warranties.

We enforce strict property protection protocols. Before beginning work, we drape landscaping and gardens in protective sheets. We build plywood shields over windows and outdoor AC units. Our crews perform detailed daily cleanups, using heavy-duty magnets to capture stray nails and fasteners.

Yes, municipal building permits are required for siding installations in most local areas, including Fort Smith and Bentonville. Ramos Roofing Plus manages the entire permitting process on your behalf, securing the permits and ensuring the installation complies with all municipal building codes.

We offer dual warranty protection: a manufacturer's material warranty and our workmanship warranty. Premium materials like James Hardie offer a 30-year non-prorated limited warranty. Our workmanship warranty protects you against installation errors, providing long-term peace of mind.
