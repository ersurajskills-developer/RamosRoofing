Diagnostic Checks

Uncovering Problems Before They Become Leaks

Whether you're buying a new home, selling a commercial property, or verifying storm damage after a storm event, a certified physical roof inspection is crucial.

Our inspectors physically climb your roof (unlike general home inspectors who often inspect from the ground with binoculars). We walk the ridges, check every valley, pull up overlapping tabs to check nails, and inspect the seals on pipe boot membranes.

After our inspection, we provide a complete visual report with annotated photos, detailing any repairs required or estimating the remaining life expectancy of the roofing system.

Inspection Checklist

  • Shingle Integrity Checks (Inspecting for lift, wear, and granule loss)
  • Flashing & Chimney Seals (Examining metal margins and lead step joints)
  • Full Visual Photo Summary (Direct evidence of current roof condition)

Roof leaks can take months to manifest internally. Hail strikes bruise the asphalt matting of shingles, causing protective mineral granules to loosen. High winds can lift shingles, breaking their sealant bond. A professional inspection detects these micro-damages early, allowing you to address repairs or file a timely insurance claim before structural rot or mold develops.

Our inspectors perform a comprehensive diagnostic check, evaluating the shingles (checking for cracks, curls, or granule loss), testing the valley liners, checking the flashing around chimneys, dormers, and vent pipes, inspecting the gutters, and assessing the attic ventilation and insulation. We compile our findings into a detailed digital report with photos.

Hail damage leaves circular, bruised indentations in the shingle where the granules have been blasted away, exposing the dark asphalt mat underneath. Wind damage causes shingles to lift, crease, or tear. Our inspectors are trained to identify these specific storm markers, helping separate storm damage from normal wear and tear to support your insurance claim.
