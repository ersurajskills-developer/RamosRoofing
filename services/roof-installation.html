Frequently Asked Questions

Roof installation refers to building a brand-new roof structure from scratch, typically on new construction homes or new commercial buildings. This includes framing, roof decking, underlayment, and the final shingles or metal panels. Roof replacement (or re-roofing), on the other hand, involves tearing off an existing, worn-out roof on an established property, repairing any damaged decking, and installing a new roofing system in its place.

A standard residential roof installation on a new construction home is completed in 1 to 2 days. The timeline depends on the roof's pitch, size, architectural style, and the chosen material. Asphalt shingles are the fastest to install, while standing seam metal roofs, TPO systems, or synthetic slate can take slightly longer due to the specialized fitting and locking mechanisms required.

For Arkansas and Oklahoma climates, standing seam metal roofs and Class 4 impact-rated shingles (like Malarkey Legacy or Bella Vista) are the most durable options. Standing seam metal offers a 50+ year operational lifespan and exceptional resistance to high winds. Class 4 shingles are engineered with polymer-modified asphalt to absorb heavy hail impacts without fracturing, protecting your home against severe spring storms.

Proper attic ventilation is critical to ensure shingles do not bake from underneath in our hot summers. We calculate the required net free ventilating area and install a balanced system using soffit vents for intake and ridge vents for exhaust. This balanced airflow lowers attic temperatures, reduces HVAC loads, prevents moisture condensation in winter, and ensures you do not void manufacturer warranties.

Yes, we install self-adhering ice and water shield membranes on all new roof installations. This waterproof barrier is applied in all high-risk leak zones, including roof valleys, eaves, around chimneys, skylights, and vent pipes. In winter, this shield prevents ice dams from backing up water under the shingles, protecting your home's framing and ceilings from internal leaks.

We monitor local weather radar constantly. Our crews only tear off sections of a roof that can be fully felted, underlayed, and waterproofed within the same workday. If sudden rain occurs, our crews immediately halt work and seal any open sections with heavy-duty, waterproof tarps and synthetic underlayment, ensuring your home's interior remains dry and protected.

Yes, municipal building permits are required for new roof installations in both Fort Smith and Bentonville. Ramos Roofing Plus manages the entire permitting process on your behalf. We secure the permits, coordinate inspections with local building officials, and ensure the entire project complies with the latest building codes and standards.
