Architectural Style

Classic Dimension and Reliable Protection

TAMKO Heritage laminated architectural shingles have been a trusted standard in the residential roofing industry for decades. They combine reliable weather protection with a classic, shadow-toned appearance that mimics natural wood shakes.

Constructed with two layers of fiberglass mat coated with weathered asphalt and protective mineral granules, Heritage shingles offer excellent durability. Their design hides minor roof deck imperfections and provides clean dimensional shadow lines.

Ramos Roofing Plus sources TAMKO Heritage shingles directly from our supply warehouse partners, providing homeowners with a reliable, cost-effective roofing system in a wide range of colors.

Frequently Asked Questions

TAMKO Heritage shingles are premium architectural asphalt shingles that offer a beautiful, wood-shake look at a cost-effective price. They feature a multi-layered, laminated design with a deep shadow line, which creates outstanding depth and dimension, making them highly popular for residential roofing projects.

TAMKO Heritage shingles are engineered to last 25 to 30 years with proper installation and attic ventilation. Their laminated fiberglass construction is designed to withstand wind gusts up to 110-130 MPH and resist thermal cracking from extreme summer heat.

TAMKO Heritage shingles carry a wind resistance rating of up to 110 MPH, which can be upgraded to 130 MPH when installed using a six-nail application pattern and TAMKO starter shingles, protecting your home against severe windstorms and gusts.

Yes, TAMKO Heritage shingles are treated with a zinc-containing granule mix that inhibits the growth of blue-green algae, preventing dark, unsightly streaks from developing on your roof deck and keeping your home's curb appeal clean.

While building codes may allow a two-layer overlay, we always recommend tearing off the old shingles down to the deck. This allows us to inspect the decking for rot, ensure a flat surface, and install high-quality synthetic underlayment, validating the manufacturer's warranty.
