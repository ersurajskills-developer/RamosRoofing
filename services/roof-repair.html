Stopping Leaks Before They Spread

Even a minor leak can lead to catastrophic mold growth, rotted wood decking, and ruined drywall inside your home. Our dedicated roof repair service is structured around rapid response, physical diagnostic inspection, and long-lasting fixes.

We specialize in locating elusive leaks that other contractors miss. Using visual physical inspections, we trace water pathways back to flashing joints, chimney margins, rusted valley tins, or deteriorated pipe boots, sealing them permanently with premium sealants and fresh flashing materials.

Key warning signs include missing, cracked, or curled shingles; exposed nails or rusty flashing; dark stains on your interior ceilings or attic walls; saggy areas on the roof deck; and an unusual amount of shingle granules in your gutters. Catching these minor issues early during a professional roof inspection prevents water from seeping into your attic and causing structural rot.

What could have been a simple flashing or shingle repair can quickly escalate into an expensive restoration project. Promptly repairing any leak is crucial to protect your home's integrity.

We provide rapid roof repair services. If a storm has blown off shingles or caused tree damage, contact us immediately. We can deploy a emergency crew to inspect the damage and install waterproof tarps to seal the roof. We then schedule a permanent repair using matching materials to restore your roof's integrity.

If your roof is relatively new and in good condition, individual missing shingles can easily be replaced. We remove the damaged shingle, repair the underlayment if needed, and nail down a matching replacement shingle. However, if the roof is old, brittle, and failing globally, replacing individual shingles is only a temporary fix, and a replacement is recommended.

Roof repair costs vary widely based on the complexity and location of the leak. Minor repairs, such as replacing a pipe boot flashing or patching a few blown-off shingles, are very affordable. Larger repairs, such as replacing valley flashings or fixing structural decking rot, require more labor. We provide clear, itemized written estimates before starting any repair work.

If the damage is caused by a sudden, covered storm event (like wind blow-offs or hail strikes), homeowner's insurance typically covers the cost of repairs. We provide detailed photo documentation and itemized estimates for your insurer, and we can assist you throughout the claim process. General wear and tear or lack of maintenance is usually not covered.

Locating a leak requires looking past the ceiling stain, as water travels along rafters before dripping. Our experienced inspectors perform a physical check of the roof deck, checking high-risk zones like valleys, chimneys, vent pipes, and skylights. We trace the path of the water from the attic up to the exterior shingles to find the exact entry point.

The most common causes of leaks are deteriorated pipe boot flashings, rusted or improperly installed valley tin, cracked flashing around chimneys or skylights, loose wind-damaged shingles, and debris buildup in gutters that causes water to back up under the eave shingles. Regular roof maintenance and gutter cleaning can prevent most leaks.
