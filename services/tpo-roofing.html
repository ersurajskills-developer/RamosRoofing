Engineered Flat Roofs

High-Performance White TPO Flat Roofing

Flat and low-slope roofs require specialized commercial materials to prevent standing water from penetrating your building. At Ramos Roofing Plus, we specialize in high-performance white TPO (Thermoplastic Polyolefin) flat roofing.

TPO sheets are mechanically fastened or fully adhered to the roof deck, with seams hot-air welded at high temperatures. This process welds the sheets together to form a continuous, watertight seal across the entire roof surface.

The bright white surface reflects solar heat, lowering building HVAC operating costs. TPO is also highly resistant to punctures, chemicals, and mold growth, making it a reliable choice for local businesses.

Frequently Asked Questions

TPO (Thermoplastic Polyolefin) is a single-ply, heat-welded white rubber membrane that is the leading choice for flat and low-slope commercial roofs. It is commonly installed on retail spaces, office buildings, warehouses, schools, and modern residential extensions, offering exceptional waterproofing and energy efficiency.

TPO sheets are rolled out over flat roof decks and welded together at the seams using specialized hot-air welders. This process fuses the sheets, forming a continuous, seamless waterproof barrier that is much stronger than glued or taped seams, preventing water from penetrating your building.

Yes, TPO is highly energy-efficient. Its bright white surface reflects up to 80% of solar heat away from your building, keeping attic spaces and roofs cool. This reduced thermal load significantly lowers your summer cooling bills and extends the operational lifespan of your building's HVAC systems.

A professionally installed TPO roof lasts 20 to 30 years with regular maintenance. It is highly resistant to chemical exposure, UV degradation, punctures, and tears. Keeping roof drains clear and addressing minor punctures promptly ensures your TPO system performs flawlessly for decades.

TPO can be attached using three methods: mechanically attached (screwed into the deck along the seams), fully adhered (glued down with heavy-duty bonding adhesives), or ballasted (laid loose and weighted down with gravel or pavers). We recommend the best attachment method based on your building's structure.

Yes, TPO is highly repairable. If a storm, wind-blown debris, or HVAC service technician punctures the membrane, our crew can weld a new patch of TPO over the damaged area. We clean the membrane, apply heat, and roll down the patch to create a seamless waterproof seal.

TPO offers superior seam strength compared to EPDM (which uses glued seams that can dry out and leak) and is much lighter and cleaner to install than Built-Up roofs. TPO's white reflective surface also makes it far more energy-efficient than dark EPDM or tar-and-gravel roofs, reducing utility costs.

Leading commercial manufacturers require regular, professional maintenance to keep their system warranties valid. Our commercial maintenance program provides the inspections, drain clearing, and checkups required to prevent ponding water and keep your TPO manufacturer's warranty secure.

TPO installation costs depend on the building's size, accessibility, the attachment method used, and whether we need to tear off an old roof or can install TPO directly over it. TPO is highly cost-effective compared to other commercial options. We provide detailed, transparent written estimates.
